RESEARCH SAMPLE

This repository contains a compact research sample prepared for a postdoctoral application to the University of Amsterdam.

The sample is meant to show three things at once: a clear R workflow from data import to analysis, transparent scope choices and measurement decisions, and careful handling of platform data in a bounded research setting.

The empirical material consists of public Facebook posts from Norwegian politicians during the first COVID-19 lockdown period in Norway, from March 12 to May 12, 2020. Using a bounded crisis window of this kind is common in COVID-19 communication research because it creates a cleaner analytical frame than treating the full pandemic as one long undifferentiated period.

The original platform export is not redistributed here. Instead, the repository includes R scripts, derived data files, figures, a presentation, and short documentation notes. That choice keeps the workflow inspectable while avoiding direct redistribution of the underlying export.

A substantial share of the coding logic and the strategy-based variables builds on work developed during the doctoral dissertation. In that sense, this repository is best read as a compact portfolio version of a broader research workflow.

The analytic sample contains 580 posts from 11 political actors. The interaction distribution is strongly skewed. Self-enhancement is the most common coded strategy. Follower size is the strongest predictor in the model outputs. The simple COVID keyword indicator does not show a clear positive association with interaction levels.
